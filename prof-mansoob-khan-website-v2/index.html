About

A career across four continents

Prof. Mohammad Mansoob Khan is an internationally recognized inorganic chemist and nanotechnologist at Universiti Brunei Darussalam. He earned his Ph.D. in Chemistry from Aligarh Muslim University, India (2002), and held academic positions in India, Ethiopia, Oman, and South Korea before joining UBD in 2015, where he was promoted to full Professor in 2023.

His research centres on the green and phytogenic synthesis of nanomaterials and the band-gap engineering of semiconductors — TiO₂, ZnO, CeO₂, SnO₂, chalcogenides, perovskites and MXenes — for visible-light-induced photocatalysis. These materials target clean water, sustainable energy, pollution control, and antimicrobial applications. He has been listed among the world's Top 2% of scientists (Stanford/Elsevier, 2021–2025) and authored the Elsevier monographs Theoretical Concepts of Photocatalysis (2023) and Photocatalysts: Synthesis and Characterization Methods (2025).

Focus

Research highlights

Green & Phytogenic Synthesis

Plant-extract routes to metal and metal-oxide nanostructures — an environmentally benign alternative to conventional chemistry, cited industrially by Sigma-Aldrich.

Visible-Light Photocatalysis

Band-gap-engineered semiconductors and nanocomposites that harvest sunlight to degrade pollutants, split water, and disinfect — beyond UV limits.

Sustainable Energy

Biohydrogen production, microbial fuel cells, and methanol-tolerant oxygen-reduction electrocatalysts based on perovskite oxides.

Nanobiotechnology

Antimicrobial, antibiofilm, wound-healing and drug-delivery applications of semiconductor and metal-sulfide nanostructures.
